Attendance data (Viz 1) — sourced from Baseball Reference historical attendance records. Total regular-season gate attendance per year. The 2020 season was cancelled due to COVID-19 and is excluded. The 2021 season operated under reduced capacity restrictions.

World Series viewership (Viz 2) — sourced from Nielsen Media Research historical ratings as reported by Sports Media Watch. Figures represent average viewers across all games of each series. Select years chosen for narrative clarity.

Vibes Above Replacement (Viz 3) — VAR is an entirely made-up metric invented by The Brofessor. The formula (HRs × Avg HR Distance × Bicep²) / League Average is napkin math. Home run counts are real (Baseball Reference). Average HR distances are real (Statcast/Baseball Savant). Bicep circumference measurements are The Brofessor's best guesses from squinting at photos. "Optimized VAR" projections are speculative nonsense based on vibes and a loose understanding of sports pharmacology. Do not cite this in your dissertation.

Recovery timelines (Viz 4) — Current recovery baselines are approximate averages based on publicly reported MLB injured list stints. The HGH-optimized projections (40-50% reductions) are The Brofessor's own bullshit, loosely inspired by clinical literature on growth hormone and tissue repair but not directly sourced from any specific study. The reduction percentages are illustrative, not clinical.

Contract waste calculations — Contract values and games played data sourced from Baseball Reference and Spotrac through the 2024 season. "Wasted" dollars assume linear per-game contract value, which is a simplification but directionally accurate.
